Kerala Scholarships

There are several Kerala scholarships are available for students to apply which are being run by the central government and the state government of Kerala. The key departments of the state government of Kerala offer scholarships for students of the state which also include the Department of Minority Welfare and the Department of Collegiate Education. The detailed list of Kerala scholarships along with their eligibility, provider information, award details, the application process, and the tentative application period are given below.

1. Minority Welfare Scholarship

Provider: Department of Minority Welfare, Government of Kerala

Eligibility:

  • Open for students who belong to Minority communities (Muslim, Christian, Parsi, Sikh, Buddhist, and Jains).
  • The applicants must be pursuing an ITI course.
  • The annual income of the family should be less than INR 8,00,000 per annum.

Awards:

  • INR 10,000 for students pursuing the 1-year course.
  • INR 20,000 for students pursuing the 2-year course.

Application Period: Between November and December

Application Process: Applicants can apply online through Minority Welfare Scholarships Portal.

2. Minority Welfare Scholarship

Provider: Department of Minority Welfare, Government of Kerala

Eligibility:

  • Open for girl students only who belong to Muslim, Latin, or converted Christian communities.
  • The applicants must be pursuing studies at graduation level or above.
  • The percentage of marks to be obtained by them in the last qualifying examination should be 50% or more.
  • The annual income of the family should be less than INR 8,00,000.
  • Students studying in self-financing colleges from merit seats can also apply.

Awards:

  • Career Assessment Test
    • For undergraduates – INR 5,000 per annum
    • For postgraduates – INR 6,000 per annum
    • For professional courses – INR 7,000 per annum
  • Hostel stipend – INR 13,000 per annum

    • Note – The hostel stipend is available only for students residing in recognized hostels.

Application Period: Between November and December

Application Process: Applicants can apply online through Minority Welfare Scholarships Portal.

3. APJ Abdul Kalam Scholarship (APJAK), Kerala

Provider: Department of Minority Welfare, Government of Kerala

Eligibility:

  • Students belonging to Minority communities (Muslims, Sikhs, Christians, Parsis, Jains, and Buddhists) can apply for this scholarship.
  • They must be studying in a government/aided polytechnic in a merit seat.
  • The annual income of the family should be less than INR 8,00,000.

Award:

INR 6,000 per annum

Application Period: Between August and November

Application Process: Applicants can apply online through Minority Welfare Scholarships Portal.
